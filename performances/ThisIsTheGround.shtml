ASM + Opera On Tap present

This Is The Ground

Opera on Tap–in partnership with The Old Stone House, The Park Slope Fifth Avenue BID, and Anti-Social Music–will stage an interactive musical walk through America's volatile political history the weekends of July 5th and July 12th entitled "This Is The Ground."

With support from famed song historian Paul Sperry, music will include a diverse array of songs spanning popular and classical styles ranging from the 1790s to now. Billed as a sight-specific, interactive musical journey mapping an American refusal to surrender, the experience has been developed and directed by renowned theatre-makers, Latrelle Bright and Jerre Dye.

Music will include compositions by William Bolcom, Margaret Bonds, Adolphus Hailstork, and Kamala Sankaram as well as folk songs, pop songs and spirituals inspired by the likes of Odetta and Patti Smith.

Featuring performances by Lindell Carter, Adrienne Danrich, Victoria Davis, Drew Fleming, Joy Jones, and Kamala Sankaram, accompanied by Christopher Berg, Brian Thompson, Kirin McElwain and Ross Wightman.

Admission will be free for all ages for this event, but attendees will need to sign up for a time slot. Registration will be available in advance as well as on location during performances. Walks will occur between the hours of 6pm and 10pm on July 5th, 6th, 12th, and 13th at The Old Stone House (336 3rd St, Brooklyn NY 11215).

To secure a spot, RSVP to operaontap@operaontap.com.

This program is supported, in part, by public funds from the New York City Department of Cultural Affairs, in partnership with the City Council.
