ASM Presents
 
Infrequent Seams Streamfest 3

Infrequent Seams brings the third iteration of the semi-annual four-day Infrequent Seams Streamfest: A four-day festival of live performances, moving image-based and electronic sound-based creative arts. The festival musical performance, but also incorporates many other disciplines as well: this includes video/film art, poetry, dance, theater, and more.

The festival is always presented online for audiences around the globe, but live performances are also available in-person for a local audience in Brooklyn, NY. This June, two days are taking place at Scholes Street Studio in Williamsburg, one day takes place at Willow Place Auditorium in Brooklyn Heights. The fourth day is fully remote for artists and audiences, and includes live telematic collaborations between artists in different locations.

RSVP, Vaccination required for in-person attendance. RSVP at infrequentseams@gmail.com

Full schedule is available at www.infrequentseamsstreamfest.com

Joy Guidry / Pauline Kim Harris
Bassoon and violin duets from these two iconoclastic instrumental innovators

Forever House: Meaghan Burke / James Moore / James Ilgenfritz / Pete Wise
Onstage footage of new songs from avant-grunge outfit Forever House, w/ Meaghan Burke (vocals/cello), James Moore (guitar), James Ilgenfritz (bass) and Pete Wise (drums).

Forbes Graham Solo
Forbes Graham performs on trumpet and laptop computer, conjuring a staggering field of glitches, splats and drones.

Ty Citerman: Bop Kabbalah+Voices LIVE (performance footage)
Recorded live in June 2021, Citerman’s trio with Sara Serpa and Judith Berkson play cutting edge Yiddish labor songs from their 2020 Infrequent Seams debut; their artistry and improvisational chops are on full display!

Ty Citerman / Jen Baker / Shayna Dunkelman
Premiering new music for electric guitar, trombone and percussion, these three wildly creative composer/improvisers will showcase their collective exploration of timbres, textures, lyrical whimsy and unapologetic noise.

AC Diamond (Anastasia Clarke solo)

Dual woodwind maestros J. D. Parran and Ras Moshe are backed up by a trio of Payton MacDonald (mallets), James Ilgenfritz (bass) and Nava Dunkelman (percussion)

Bob Kenny (cinema) and Teerapat Parnmongkol (poetry)
Kenny and Parnmongkol present Eye (2022/2565): “As we stare at the abyss and the abyss will answer back to you. Within the eye we see the world and we see ourselves.”

Anagram Ensemble Presents: Josh Sinton / Jonathan Finlayson
Anagram Ensemble presents two ubiquitous voices in New York’s creative music scene: Josh Sinton (woodwinds) and Jonathan Finlayson (trumpet), playing new duo works.

Monika Weiss METAMORPHOSIS | NIRBHAYA “Metá”
Meta is part of Polish-American media artist Monika Weiss’ cycle of five sound compositions and silent films titled Metamorphosis | Nirbhaya (2021-2022). Directed, composed, choreographed, and edited by the artist.

Muyassar Kurdi Solo
Interdisciplinary performer Muyassar Kurdi uses voice and electronics to stir a plethora of emotions from her audience through vicious noise, ritualistic chants, and meditative movements.

Julie Herndon: “At That Time”
“At That Time” Is a video opera that draws its narrative from history, presenting a richly nuanced retelling of the lives of modernist writer Gertrude Stein and her partner Alice B. Toklas.

Anagram Ensemble: Margaret Lancastesr & Chris Nappi Anagram Ensemble flutist and percussionist Margaret Lancaster and Chris Nappi perform solos and duos, including works by Elizabeth Hoffman, Jo Kondo, and Belinda Reynolds.

Davor Vincze: “Xinsheng”
Davor Vincze’s video opera “Xinsheng” is set in a quasi-dystopian world of tangled relationships, cutthroat competition, and medical malpractice.

Jessica Ackerley / JI / Eli Wallace / percussion TBD

Anagram String Trio: Works by Niloufar Shiri & Benjamin Patterson
Violist Stephanie Griffin, cellist Meaghan Burke, and bassist James Ilgenfritz perform works by composer Niloufar Shiri and FLUXUS artist Benjamin Patterson

Hypersurface: Drew Wesely / Lester St. Louis / Carlo Costa
Hypersurface is a trio of three excellent young luminaries Drew Wesley (guitar), Lester St. Louis (cello), and Carlo Costa (percussion) who offer a fresh articulation in the creative music tradition.

Tom Law / Kevin Cheli / Sandy Ewen
Textured clouds of sound swell and expand as scintillating structures float above a boundless expanse of sea and sky -- with Kevin Cheli (percussion), Tom Law (Max/MSP), and Sandy Ewen (video).

Jen Kutler & Quintan Ana Wikswo
A wife-and-wife duo, sound artist and engineer JEN KUTLER builds body-worn electronics that sonify empathy; vocalist and lyricist QUINTAN ANA WIKSWO creates strategies and tactics for amplifying silenced human rights abuses and injustices.
